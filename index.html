You Matter

Resources for Mental Health & Therapy


Hey there!

Thanks for stopping by. Here, you can find a collection of mental health resources put together by Emma and her community. Whether you need to talk someone right now or are just looking to get started with therapy, we've got you covered.

Each hotline offers one-click contact; you can open a new email message or dial a phone number just by clicking on it. If you want to explore these resources more or access features that aren't linked in, such as online chat, just click on the hotline's name to go to the most relevant area of its website.

If you need to access these resources without others in your household knowing, make sure to disable any logging software on your computer and clear your search history once you're finished.

If your life is in immediate danger, dial 911 or your country's emergency response number.

Lastly, thanks for taking the time to look through this page. Don't forget that you're worth it, and even though things might suck right now, you've got Emma and the Bloat Squad in your corner.


United States Hotlines

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

1 (800) 273-8255

"The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline provides free and confidential support for people in distress, prevention and crisis resources for you or your loved ones, and best practices for professionals." Available 24/7 via phone or online chat.

The Trevor Project

1 (866) 488-7386
Text START to 678678

"The Trevor Project is the leading national organization providing crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer & questioning youth." Available 24/7 via phone, text, or online chat.

Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual, and Transgender National Hotline

Hotline: 1 (888) 843-4564
Youth Talkline: 1 (800) 246-7743
Senior Helpline: 1 (888) 234-7243
help@LGBThotline.org

"The LGBT National Help Center serves gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, and questioning people by providing free and confidential peer support and local resources." Available on a varying schedule via phone or online chat.

National Domestic Violence Hotline

1 (800) 799-7233
Text START to 1 (800) 799-7233

"The National Domestic Violence Hotline is available for anyone experiencing domestic violence, seeking resources or information, or questioning unhealthy aspects of their relationship." Available 24/7 via phone, text, or online chat.

Rape, Abuse, and Incest National Network (RAINN)

1 (800) 656-4673

"RAINN (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network) is the nation’s largest anti-sexual violence organization. RAINN created and operates the National Sexual Assault Hotline in partnership with more than 1,000 local sexual assault service providers across the country and operates the DoD Safe Helpline for the Department of Defense. RAINN also carries out programs to prevent sexual violence, help survivors, and ensure that perpetrators are brought to justice." Available 24/7 via phone or online chat.

Your Life Your Voice Helpline

1 (800) 448-3000
Text VOICE to 20121 (hours for text service vary)

"Your Life Your Voice is a program of Boystown USA and is available to children, parents, and families who are struggling with self-harm, mental health disorders, and abuse." Available 24/7 via phone, text, email, or online chat.

National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) Helpline

1 (800) 950-6264
info@nami.org

"The National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) Helpline assists individuals and families who have questions about mental health disorders, treatment, and support services." Available Mon-Fri, 10:00am to 6:00pm Eastern Standard Time via phone or email.

National Runaway Safeline

1 (800) 786-2929
info@1800runaway.org

"The National Runaway Safeline provides crisis and support services for homeless and runaway youth in the United States." Available 24/7 via phone, email, forums, or online chat.

Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) Helpline

1 (800) 662-4357

"SAMHSA’s National Helpline provides free and confidential treatment, referral, and information services for individuals and families facing mental and/or substance abuse disorders." Available 24/7 via phone in both English and Spanish.

WomensLaw

Web Form

"The WomensLaw online helpline provides basic legal information, referrals, and emotional support for survivors of abuse." Questions are submitted via a form embedded in their website and answered in 1–5 business days via your choice of their website and/or email.

ChildHelp National Child Abuse Hotline

1 (800) 422-4453
Text: 1 (800) 442-4453

"The Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline is dedicated to the prevention of child abuse. Serving the U.S. and Canada, the hotline is staffed 24 hours a day, 7 days a week with professional crisis counselors who—through interpreters—provide assistance in over 170 languages. The hotline offers crisis intervention, information, and referrals to thousands of emergency, social service, and support resources. All calls are confidential." Available 24/7 via phone, text, or online chat.

National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC)

1 (800) 843-5678

"NCMEC serves as a clearinghouse and comprehensive reporting center for all issues related to the prevention of and recovery from child victimization." Available at various hours via phone or tipline.

National Human Trafficking Hotline

1 (888) 373-7888
Text: 233733

"The National Human Trafficking Hotline is a national anti-trafficking hotline serving survivors of human trafficking and the anti-trafficking community in the United States. The toll-free hotline is available to answer calls from anywhere in the country, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, every day of the year in more than 200 languages." Available 24/7 via phone or text in many languages.


International Hotlines

Crisis Text Line

United States & Canda: Text HOME to 741741
United Kingdom: Text HOME to 85258
Ireland: Text HOME to 50808

"Crisis Text Line is here for any crisis. A live, trained Crisis Counselor receives the text and responds, all from our secure online platform. The volunteer Crisis Counselor will help you move from a hot moment to a cool moment." Available 24/7 via text in the United States, Canda, the United Kingdom, and Ireland.

For Americans currently abroad.

Learn about Mental Health

Social Work License Map

Like a linktree for United States mental health resources

"Mental health is an undeniably important matter, yet most people don’t have access to the resources they need when they need help. At Social Work License Map, we have compiled a comprehensive list of resources for anyone seeking information about and/or help for a range of mental health issues.

Our list spans diagnostic tools, research portals, government organizations, nonprofits, blogs, and phone hotlines devoted to addressing issues ranging from general mental illnesses and disorders such as autism, anxiety, depression, eating disorders, and substance abuse, to the specific needs of diverse populations, including LGBTQ youth, minorities, women, and veterans. Take a look at these resources, and we hope you find our list helpful!"

Rethink.org

Plain-language explanations of mental health conditions

"If you have a mental health condition, your doctor will give you a formal diagnosis. While most people experience mental health conditions in very different ways, most people will share some common symptoms and side-effects."

This isn't the most scientific source, and its grammar leaves plenty to be desired. But it does a good job of breaking many of the most common mental health conditions down into concise summaries that you don't need a medical degree to understand. It also has an extensive list of hotlines in the UK.

MedlinePlus: U.S. National Library of Medicine

Comprehensive government source on all things mental health

"Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act as we cope with life. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood and aging.

Mental disorders are serious conditions which can affect your thinking, mood, and behavior. They may be occasional or long-lasting. They can affect your ability to relate to others and function each day. Mental disorders are common; more than half of all Americans will be diagnosed with one at some time in their life. But there are treatments. People with mental disorders can get better, and many of them recover completely."

This source has lots of Spanish language content!

Rise Above the Disorder

For Everyone at Low or No Cost

"Rise Above the Disorder (RAD) began as a video game group dedicated to helping gamers. Today, it's a non-profit dedicated to removing the two greatest barriers to mental health care: access and affordability. To accomplish our mission, we provide a universal mental health care system to people around the world.

The journey towards mental wellness can be long and challenging. You may have found this page at your darkest hour, at the height of motivation, or with great dedication to someone else's wellbeing; regardless, the path to healing begins here."
