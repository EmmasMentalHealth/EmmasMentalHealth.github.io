You Matter

Resources for Mental Health & Therapy

Hey there!

Thanks for stopping by. Here, you can find a collection of mental health resources put together by Emma and her community. Whether you need to talk someone right now or are just looking to get started with therapy, we've got you covered.

Each hotline offers one-click contact; you can open a new email message or dial a phone number just by clicking on it. If you want to explore these resources more or access features that aren't linked in, such as online chat, just click on the hotline's name to go to the most relevant area of its website.

If you need to access these resources without others in your household knowing, make sure to disable any logging software on your computer and clear your search history once you're finished.

If your life is in immediate danger, dial 911 or your country's emergency response number.

Lastly, thanks for taking the time to look through this page. Don't forget that you're worth it, and even though things might suck right now, you've got Emma and the Bloat Squad in your corner.



General Mental Health Hotlines

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Phone: 988
Text: 988
OG Number: 1 (800) 273-8255
En Español: 1 (888) 628-9454
Deaf & Hard of Hearing TTY: Use your preferred relay service or dial 711 then 988

Formerly known as the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline.

Available in the United States 24/7 via phone, text, and online chat.

Offers help in English and en Español, as well as Deaf- & Hard of Hearing-accessible resources (TTY).

"If you’re thinking about suicide, are worried about a friend or loved one, or would like emotional support, the Lifeline network is available 24/7 across the United States."
-The 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline's website

Crisis Text Line

United States: Text HOME to 741741
Canada: Text HOME to 686868
United Kingdom: Text HOME to 85258
Ireland: Text HOME to 50808

Available in the United States, Canda, the United Kingdom, and Ireland 24/7 via text, online chat, and WhatsApp.

"Crisis Text Line is here for any crisis. A live, trained Crisis Counselor receives the text and responds, all from our secure online platform. The volunteer Crisis Counselor will help you move from a hot moment to a cool moment."
-The Crisis Text Line's website

Your Life Your Voice Helpline

Phone: 1 (800) 448-3000
Text VOICE to 20121 (Hours for text service vary.)

Available 24/7 via phone, text, email, and online chat.

"Your Life Your Voice is a program of Boystown USA and is available to children, parents, and families who are struggling with self-harm, mental health disorders, and abuse."
-Your Life Your Voice's website

Phone: 1 (800) 950-6264
Text HelpLine to 62640
Email: helpline@nami.org

Available Mon-Fri, 10:00am to 10:00pm U.S. Eastern Time (see current U.S. Eastern Time here) via phone, text, email, and online chat.

"The National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) Helpline assists individuals and families who have questions about mental health disorders, treatment, and support services."

Vibrant Emotional Health

Operates a wide variety of mental and emotional health resources in the United States
"We run innovative community programs for people at all stages of life, and state-of-the-art crisis lines. ... As leaders, advocates, educators, and innovators in mental health, we have been raising awareness and offering support to everyone who is struggling. We work every single day to help save lives and assist people to get care anytime, anywhere and in any way that works for them. We are unwavering in our belief that everyone can achieve emotional wellness with the right care and support."
-Vibrant Emotional Health's website

This source contains a catalog of wellness resources, featuring both mainstream hotlines and smaller, more community-driven initiatives that don't have their own listings here on You Matter. Some of their more unique offerings include Disaster Distress resources, ASL (American Sign Language) resources, and the NYC Well program, which serves New York City.

Online screenings for veterans

Available in the United States 24/7 via its website.

"Behavioral health—which includes mental health, substance use, and more—is a key part of your overall well-being. Brief screenings are the quickest way to determine if you or someone you care about should connect with a behavioral health professional.

Learn About Mental Health

Social Work License Map

Like a linktree for United States mental health resources

"Mental health is an undeniably important matter, yet most people don’t have access to the resources they need when they need help. At Social Work License Map, we have compiled a comprehensive list of resources for anyone seeking information about and/or help for a range of mental health issues.

Our list spans diagnostic tools, research portals, government organizations, nonprofits, blogs, and phone hotlines devoted to addressing issues ranging from general mental illnesses and disorders such as autism, anxiety, depression, eating disorders, and substance abuse, to the specific needs of diverse populations, including LGBTQ youth, minorities, women, and veterans. Take a look at these resources, and we hope you find our list helpful!"
-Social Work License Map's website


Rethink.org

Plain-language explanations of mental health conditions
"If you have a mental health condition, your doctor will give you a formal diagnosis. While most people experience mental health conditions in very different ways, most people will share some common symptoms and side-effects."
-Rethink.org
This isn't the most scientific source, but it does a good job of breaking down many of the most common mental health conditions into concise summaries that you don't need a medical degree to understand. It also has an extensive list of hotlines in the UK.
-The You Matter Team

MedlinePlus: U.S. National Library of Medicine

Comprehensive government source on all things mental health

¡Hay mucho contenido en Español aquí!
(There's lots of content in Spanish here!)

"Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act as we cope with life. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood and aging.

Mental disorders are serious conditions which can affect your thinking, mood, and behavior. They may be occasional or long-lasting. They can affect your ability to relate to others and function each day. Mental disorders are common; more than half of all Americans will be diagnosed with one at some time in their life. But there are treatments. People with mental disorders can get better, and many of them recover completely."
-MedlinePlus' website


211.org

Don't know what to try? Calling 211 can help.

This service is available in the United States and Canada.

"211 connects you to expert, caring help. Every call is completely confidential."
-211.org
Dialing 211 will connect you to a call center than can refer you to resources and organizations that offer help with addiction, disasters, emergency food and financial assistance, career counseling, therapy, suicide prevention, and more.
-The You Matter Team
